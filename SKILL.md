# Inverted Pyramid Tech Writing

Use this skill when writing or rewriting technical blog content so the reader gets the answer immediately and can stop at any point without missing the core point.

## Outcome

Produce technical writing that is ordered by descending importance:

1. Main answer and thesis
2. Key supporting points
3. Background and context
4. Nice-to-know details

The first paragraph should work as a standalone summary of the article.

## Workflow

1. Identify the core question the article answers.
2. Write a one-sentence answer to that question before drafting anything else.
3. Identify the two to four supporting points that best defend the answer.
4. Order those points by importance, not by chronology.
5. Put definitions, history, side notes, and broader context after the main answer and supporting points.

## Writing Rules

- The title should state the claim or topic directly. Avoid teaser titles.
- The opening paragraph should answer the core question immediately.
- If the article centers on a small set of items, name them in the opening paragraph.
- The second paragraph should summarize why those items matter before any background section appears.
- The first section after the lead should expand the most important point, not the background.
- Each section should earn its place by answering: "Does the reader need this before they can leave the page?"
- Keep supporting examples close to the point they support.
- Push history, caveats, and comparisons lower unless they are required to understand the answer.
- For technical posts, prefer explicit claims over atmospheric introductions.
- Do not spend the early part of the article defending why the question is interesting. Answer it first.

## Technical Post Pattern

Use this default shape unless the user wants something else:

1. Title
2. Lead paragraph with the direct answer
3. Short scan-friendly paragraph or list naming the main points if the set is small and concrete
4. Section for the most important point
5. Section for the second most important point
6. Section for the third most important point
7. Short section for secondary or supporting Git, engineering, or workflow details
8. Closing that restates the conclusion briefly

For ranked technical pieces, expand the top items before adding background, comparisons, or broader interpretation.

## What To Demote

Move these lower in the article unless the user explicitly wants a background-first piece:

- long scene-setting intros
- historical framing
- broad comparisons
- exhaustive side features
- philosophy about the topic

## Self-Check

Before finalizing, verify:

- The first paragraph answers the article's core question.
- A reader who stops after the first two paragraphs still gets the argument and the main set of items.
- The most important section appears before background.
- Supporting details do not bury the main answer.
- Nice-to-know material is lower than must-know material.
